Personal

Howdy! As said before, my name is Maggie von Ebers, I'm a sophomore computer science major, and I am the loudest and the proudest member of the fighting Texas Aggies Class of 2020 AY-AY-AY-AY!

I'm Des Moines, Iowa born and raised, though I have also lived in Rochester, New York and Fargo, North Dakota (where I now live when not at school). I have no connections whatsoever to Texas, but I was drawn to Aggieland's collaborative spirit, traditions, and of course the football games!

When I'm not working on a coding project or homework, you can find me reading, sketching, or riding horses. I love being an equestrian so much that I've ridden competitively at events across the nation for over ten years. When I moved from New York to North Dakota, I found that there was no competition scene there. Thanks to online classes and plenty of saved frequent-flier miles I was able to fly back and forth from ND to my horse and trainer in NY; I did this for all four years of highschool.

This time spent traveling is what I think gave me my start in computer science. On airplanes you can't access much of the internet, so I began to dig through Windows' file systems and gain a curiousity for all that went on beneath the GUI. After I found this love, I was lucky enough to be accepted to Stanford's Pre-Collegiate program studying Artificial Intelligence. That's when I knew I couldn't do anything else with my life!

These days my main focus and interest is in machine learning and natural language processing, though I'd like to see what else the underlying theory can apply to.
